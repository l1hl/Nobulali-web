Nobulali, the Artist

The world of Social Science has opened innumerable opportunities for me to use many of my talents. Now, I work with organisations to strengthen their team functionality and synergy. I am passionate about and have enjoyed bringing creativity and experiential learning to my work, be it with the Education sector, Corporations, NGOs, or Government entities.

In my Teaching, Consulting, Presenting, Producing, Directing, Dancing, Applied Theatre, and Singing, I am committed to delivering with excellence. I hope to work or partner with you.

When I am not busy with social change projects, you can find me mountain climbing, skydiving, skiing, kickboxing, or dancing.

One of the hats I am proud to wear is that of an Educator. I use my background in the Arts to inform my teaching, research, and facilitation of workshops. I am currently a lead faculty at the Gordon Institute of Business Science (GIBS), University of Pretoria. I have also taught the Warwick Business School Executive MBA and was a sessional lecturer with Queen Mary University of London (School of Business Management & School of English and Drama), and University of Witwatersrand. My expertise as well as teaching and research interests cuts across Corporate Strategy, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, Entrepreneurship, Experiential Learning, Behavioural Insights (Nudge), Symbolic Interaction, Leadership, Performance, Drama, and Applied Theatre.

I am a Qualitative Researcher with a particular interest in video methods. I aspire to encourage emerging researchers and have begun supervising masters and PhD level, towards this end. I also plan to use the arts as a non-medical response and solution to mental health, specifically using social prescribing. I also hold a Doctor of Philosophy from Warwick Business School. My dissertation was an exploration of discursive, material and embodiment in strategy meetings.

I am the Founder and Managing Director of ShakeXperience ®, an Applied Theatre company best known for the award-winning educational programme and our African adaptation of George Orwell's Animal Farm. I am also the Co-Founder of Nudgeathon™ (Nudgeathon Fellow) at the Warwick Business School, working with partners from the UK to South Africa, Mexico, Australia, and Kuwait among others. I dream big and I dream impact – and these two initiatives speak to these very qualities. As a founder, co-founder, and managing director, I have developed a knack for designing, organising, and executing workshops and training. It would be a great honour to work with your teams towards greater effectiveness and output

My career journey started on stage and writing for the stage, later joining film and television. My break-out role was playing ‘Lulu’ in Sophiatown, directed by Malcolm Purkey at The Market Theatre. This role earned me a Naledi Award for Best Newcomer. Since then, I have had the privilege of presenting two youth television shows, Take 5 and Iketsetse. I then played the role of ‘Thuliswe’ in the SABC 3 drama series Hard Copy III, ‘Yoli’ in the second and third seasons of the SABC 1 drama series Home Affairs, and ‘Thuli’ in the SABC 1 mini-series A Place Called Home.

I also played the role of a drug dealer's daughter ‘Nkhensani Tshinawa’ in the e.tv soapie, Rhythm City, and the role of ‘Cynthia’ in a movie called The Bet, directed by Hlomla Dandala for Curious Pictures. As an actor, I have also dabbled in television commercials for MTN and LoveLife, and I have a plethora of voice overs for radio PSAs under my belt. The stage is a special place, and one where I feel quite at home.
